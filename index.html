ENT 540 · Negotiation — Sources & Uses of Power

15 weeks · 2×/week · 90 minutes each · Undergraduate
Entrepreneurship / Startups Focus

Instructor: Domingos Santos · Email/Office Hours: TBA

Course outcomes
  • Apply BATNA/ZOPA, value creation/claiming frameworks.
  • Diagnose and use sources of power ethically.
  • Negotiate co-founder splits, term sheets, sales, hiring, vendors.
  • Manage conflict; run multi-party negotiations.
  • Reflect on style, biases, and ethics.

Course Description

Entrepreneurial success hinges on the ability to negotiate and wield power ethically in high-stakes, high-uncertainty environments. Through interactive lectures, role-play simulations, and top-tier cases, you’ll build a practical toolkit for startup negotiations with co-founders, investors, customers, hires, and partners.

We emphasize principled, creative, and ethical agreements that build trust and long-term value.

Meeting Pattern

Weeks: 15
Sessions: Twice per week
Length: 90 minutes per class
Assessment: Participation, reflection journals, case memos, in-class simulations, and a major final project (no exam).

Weekly Schedule (Topics & Activities)

Week 1 • Intro to Startup Negotiation & Power

Definitions, the 7-Element framework; diagnostic role-play.

Week 2 • Distributive Bargaining (Claiming Value)

Targets, reservation price, ZOPA, anchoring; Bullard Houses simulation.

Week 3 • Integrative Bargaining (Creating Value)

Interests vs. positions, option generation, packages; New Recruit multi-issue role-play.

Week 4 • Sources of Power & Ethical Influence

Bases of power; Cialdini’s principles; framing & persuasion drills.

Week 5 • Styles, Emotions, & Biases

Style self-assessment; Beyond Reason core concerns; bias traps.

Week 6 • Co-Founder Agreements & Equity

Equity splits, roles, vesting; HBS case + simulated founders’ negotiation.

Week 7 • Fundraising & Term Sheets

Valuation vs. control; contingent contracts; Aerospace Investment simulation.

Week 8 • Sales & Corporate Partnerships

Negotiating customer pilots, alliances; “dancing with elephants.”

Week 9 • Hiring & Talent Offers

Cash/equity compensation, role design; candidate-founder role-play.

Week 10 • Suppliers & Cross-Cultural Deals

Manufacturing/vendors; cultural dimensions; MedLee JV simulation.

Week 11 • Conflict & Difficult Conversations

Founders/board conflict; mediation; Getting Past No tactics.

Week 12 • Multi-Party & Team Negotiations

Coalitions, agenda setting, caucuses; team-on-team simulation.

Week 13 • Ethics, Trust, & Power

Boundaries of puffery vs. deception; negotiator’s code of ethics.

Week 14 • Synthesis & Project Workshop

Concept review; project coaching and rehearsal.

Week 15 • Final Project Presentations

Team presentations / live mini-sims; course wrap.

Assignments & Grading

  • Reflective Journals (3) – 15%
  • Case Memos (2) – 15%
